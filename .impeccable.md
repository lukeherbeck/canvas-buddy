## Design Context

### Users
College students (Texas A&M focus initially) using Canvas LMS daily for assignments, quizzes, and deadlines. They're stressed but also gamers — they grew up on mobile games, Duolingo streaks, and Pokémon. They open Canvas with dread; CanvasBuddy should flip that into a moment of delight. Context: floating panel overlay on the left side of any Canvas page, seen multiple times per day.

### Brand Personality
**Hype. Slick. Surprising.**

Not messy-hype — *satisfyingly* loud. The energy of a well-made mobile game: every interaction feels good, every reward feels earned, every animation has snap. Like Pokémon meets a premium productivity tool. The buddy is a character, not a widget.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary palette**: Amber gold (#f59e0b / #fbbf24) as the XP/reward/fire color. Hot coral (#f43f5e) for urgency and action. Dark galaxy (#0f0c29 → #1e1b4b) for the buddy zone — a window into space inside the panel.
- **Panel shell**: Warm cream glass (rgba(255, 253, 245, 0.92)) with backdrop-filter blur. Light, not dark — the glass contrasts with the galaxy buddy area below.
- **Typography**: Bebas Neue for display numbers and titles (level badge, panel name) — condensed, bold, game-like. Nunito for all body/friendly text — rounds the edges, keeps it warm.
- **Motion**: Spring easing everywhere (cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)). Most UI is clean and restrained. Key moments go full drama: level-up triggers particle sparks + radial flash + sprite shake. XP bar glows amber when near threshold.
- **Anti-references**: No indigo/violet gradients on white (the classic AI slop look). No Notion/Linear/Todoist muted productivity aesthetic. Not childish — college students, not kids.

### Design Principles
1. **The buddy area is sacred** — the dark galaxy zone is visually distinct from the rest of the panel. It signals "you're entering the game layer." Never let it feel like a widget.
2. **Earn the drama** — the shell is clean and restrained so that level-up moments hit hard by contrast. Not everything glows; the moments that do feel special.
3. **Amber = achievement** — every XP bar, level badge, points chip, and reward signal uses amber/gold. It's the reward color. Consistent, Pavlovian.
4. **Coral = urgency** — overdue items, CTAs, and high-stakes moments use hot coral. Students know immediately something needs attention.
5. **Crisp over cluttered** — 248px wide panel. Information density must be tight but never crowded. Every element earns its space.
